Section 2(d): The New "Document"

The BSA 2023 shatters the paper-first barrier. A "document" now explicitly includes:

  • Digital Records: Server logs, cloud snapshots, and blockchain ledgers.
  • Communication: Emails, WhatsApp messages, and encrypted signal logs.
  • IoT Data: Smartwatch pings and GPS telemetry are now "Substantial Evidence."

Section 2(e): Evidence Redefined

Evidence is now categorized to embrace the digital revolution:

Oral Evidence

Includes statements made via video-conferencing, effectively ending the need for physical presence in remote cases.

Documentary Evidence

Includes all electronic records which are now treated as primary documents if the conditions of Section 57/63 are met.

The Data-First Philosophy

Unlike the IEA 1872, which viewed digital data as an 'exception' (Section 65B), the BSA 2023 treats digital data as the Standard. The foundation rests on Integrity over Ink.

Hash Values

The mathematical signature (SHA-256) of a file is now a statutory requirement to prove non-alteration from seizure to courtroom.

Metadata Sovereignty

The "Date Modified" and "Owner" logs of a file are no longer ancillary; they are core facts that prove the timing of a crime.

Digital Breadcrumbs & Conduct

Modern relevancy isn't just about what a person said, but what their devices recorded before and after the event.

Section 4: Digital Res Gestae

+

Logs recorded automatically by a system during a transaction (e.g., a stock market trade or a smart-lock entry) are part of the transaction and admissible without independent witness testimony.

Section 6: Digital Preparation

+

Search histories (e.g., "how to hide a body") or the download of wiping software (e.g., CCleaner) just before a crime is "Conduct" that proves preparation and guilty mind.

Section 9: The Digital Alibi

+

A plea of alibi is established through "Incompatibility." If your device pings a tower 50km away from the crime scene at the exact time of the incident, your presence at the scene becomes "Highly Improbable."

Admissibility Gateway: Section 63

The BSA mandates a clear path for every computer output (printouts, CDs, drives). To be admissible, a certificate must prove:

  1. Proper Operation: The computer was operating correctly during the data creation.
  2. Lawful Control: The person producing the record had lawful access to the device.
  3. Integrity: The contents were not altered during the storage or retrieval process.

🚨 Warning: Without a Section 63 Certificate, the most damning digital evidence is legally invisible (void) in Indian courts.

Presuming Technical Truth

To speed up trials, the BSA grants "Presumption Status" to secure digital processes. This shifts the burden of proof from the prosecution to the defense.

Section 86: Integrity

If a record is stored in a "Secure System" (Hashed/Encrypted), the court Shall Presume that the record has not been altered.

Section 85: Agreements

Digital signatures on contracts Shall be Presumed genuine, treating them with the same weight as physical signatures.

The End of the 30-Year Rule

Old paper documents benefited from a 30-year presumption of genuineness. In the digital age, hardware fails and formats change much faster.

BSA Section 93: For electronic records, the rule is reduced to 5 Years. If a record is 5 years old and in proper custody, the court may presume its genuineness.

The Rise of the Scientific Witness

Section 39 of the BSA elevates the role of the Cyber Expert. Their opinion is now vital for:

  • Deepfake Verification: Detecting AI-generated audio and video.
  • Encryption Breaking: Lawful bypass of device security for investigation.
  • Cloud Forensics: Analyzing data that never physically touched a local drive.
